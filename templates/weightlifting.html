How lifting weights changed my life

"No man has the right to be an amateur in the matter of physical training. It is a shame for a man to grow old without seeing the beauty and strength of which his body is capable.‚Äù- Socrates

Growing up i was always the small weak kid amongst my friends and peers from school, and somehow i got used to being like this lazy,unproductive and weak.

In the beginning of the pandemic i wasted much time playing video games, eating junk and not exercsing at all, that led me to being in the worst shape of my life.

So I made a change to my lifestyle and decided that i will start working out ,eat clean and learn about it as well, through the internet.

I remember spending hours watching fitness videos, nutrition videos and finally after understanding some of it, i started implementing what i had learned.

In the beginnig it was extremely tough, counting my food intake/calories, then resisiting urges to eat junk,forcing myself to workout even on the days i didn't feel like it

But once enough time passed by and i started seeing the results, i felt like it was all worth it. Even though i have just begun, I dont feel like stopping now and letting myself fall back onto my old habits.

I hope this gives someone the motivation and urge to start atleast exercising and staying consistent with it and see the magic it does to yourself...

Some tips if you are just starting out

  • Eat enough according to your goals, especially eat your protein!
  • Lift weights with correct form and technique and dont be afraid to ask people for help.
  • Dont go on crazy fad diets which are extremely low in calories and have no nutritional value.
  • Always keep learning new things about your workout,nutrition.
  • Get your fitness information from good and trusted sources which are backed scientifically.

What I did personally that helped me get bigger

  • Ate 2g Protein per Kg of my bodyweight
  • Ate in a caloric surplus of 250-300
  • Progressively got stronger on my main lifts
  • Trained until muscle failure almost everyday
  • Tried getting atleast 7-8 hours of sleep daily
